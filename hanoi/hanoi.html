Hanoi

Somehow, in the middle of that living,
breathing, beating city,
an inchworm
on my cheekbone.

I wondered how,
for a moment,
but I knew.

It was living, breathing, beating— same as I.

That summer, I watched the city lights float by
on a plane ride back from Austin,
tiny orange lights like fish eggs.

A whole town anonymous.
Living, breathing, beating,
maybe.

That was the summer Tristan died,
though it was months before I said it.

Tonight, the rain drips down like footsteps
in a world no longer his.

Somewhere, the streetlights are flickering
on, a stoplight is turning yellow,
a houseplant is turning brown.

I guess to be alive
is just to be alive
and that’s enough.
